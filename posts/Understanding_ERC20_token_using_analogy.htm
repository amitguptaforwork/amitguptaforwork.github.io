How to think about ERC20 token

Imagine that I am God and I want to sell tokens. I appoint Contractor Ravi who has a notepad (Contractor Ravi is the ERC20 token contract). Contractor Ravi is having a bunch of gold coins in his pocket (actually he does not have physical coins, but he just says he has some coins with some totalSupply). Contractor Ravi will note down the names of owners and how much coins are owned by each owner (the data structures in ERC20 hold address to token count mapping). Initially when I give the book to Contractor Ravi, I initialize how many coins would be therefor this, I tell Contractor Ravi how many coins to mint and give my address, so all these coins are owned by me. (this is done in the constructor where we call the _mint internal function of ERC20.sol, pass it value of initial supply and msg.sender)

 

 

 

 

 

 

Contractor Ravis main job is to coordinate buying and selling of tokens ad infinite. To sell in market though, I appoint another guy, Salesman Kavi for selling the token( token sale contract of an ICO). Salesman Kavi creates a website, does all the promotions etc. On the day of the sale, people login to the website, pay in ETH and hope to get tokens. I have my account key configured in this website, so that when somebody pays money to the website, I can transfer some of my coins to the buyer. Salesman Kavis website accepts ETH payments. The website also calculates how much tokens will be given, based on a predefined sale price. Then it talks to Contractor Ravi, with my account signing the txn (so that I am the msg.sender) and passes the buyer address and token amount . Contractor Ravi checks that the msg.sender has enough tokens, takes out his book, notes the new buyer name and transfers tokens to new buyer from my account (contract transfers token from msg.sender (contract owner in this case) account to new buyer account). Anybody can check Contractor Ravis book and therefore confirm that who has how many tokens. Salesman Kavi is doing what is called ICO sale.

 

 

 

 

After a while, ICO method becomes infamous so nowadays people are going for direct listing on DEX this is called IDO. Lets say DEX name is Exchange Berodha. I give Exchange Berodha 1000 coins and 1000 rupees and therefore, say that 1 coin = 1 rupee. This sets the price of the coin on Exchange Berodhas website ( DEX asks the coin creator to create a liquidity pool for the new coin. The price of all coins is considered equal to amount of ETH provided and this gives the initial price of the coin! So if we gave $1000 worth of ether, and we gave 100 coins, then each coin is listed for ETH that is worth $10 ($1000/100). Here again, Contractor Ravi notes in his books that Exchange Berodha owns 100 coins. As people buy from Exchange Berodha, Exchange Berodha passes this info to Contractor Ravi. Contractor Ravi reduces Exchange Berodhas account and increases user account.

 

 

 

 

 

 

If the coin really takes off, there are others willing to buy. As they buy from Exchange Berodha, the price of the coin goes up (as the supply got reduced (constant product AMM model)thus price increases.if coin gets more traction, then there are others who want to give their coins to Exchange Berodha for the liquidity pool (liquidity providers) in hope of making a return through fees. As the amount of coins increase decrease in pool, the price fluctuates. At a later point, I can also sell my tokens and make a cool sum of money. There may be investors whom I may have sold some tokens initially, for funding purposes. They can also offload their stash of coins on the DEX. In order to make sure not all the owners and investors dump their coins on Exchange Berodha as soon as trading starts, usually there would be a lock-in period (vesting period) before these owners can sell their tokens.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Sometimes as owner, I want to authorise others to spend some amount on my behalf. In this case, I can inform Contractor Ravi address of the spender and how much amount I want them to be able to spend out of my total amount.

I may choose to make Contractor Ravi more sophisticated. Like I can pause sale of any tokens, blacklist or whitelist addresses, burn or mint tokens (which reduces or increases total supply) etc.
